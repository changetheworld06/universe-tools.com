Celsius vs Fahrenheit: what’s the difference?

Celsius (°C) is commonly used in most countries and is based on water’s freezing point (0°C) and boiling point (100°C) at standard pressure. Fahrenheit (°F) is mainly used in the United States; water freezes at 32°F and boils at 212°F.

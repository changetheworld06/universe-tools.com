Energy converter: what these units mean and when to use them

This energy unit converter helps you switch between Joules (J), kilocalories (kcal) and kilowatt-hours (kWh). These three units are used in different contexts: joules for science and engineering, kilocalories for nutrition and sport tracking, and kilowatt-hours for electricity consumption and billing. Being able to convert between them makes it easier to compare “food energy” with “electrical energy” and understand what a value represents.

The tool uses fixed conversion factors. Because 1 kcal = 4,184 J, you can convert kcal to joules by multiplying by 4,184, and joules to kcal by dividing by 4,184. For electricity, 1 kWh = 3,600,000 J. This comes from the definition of a watt (1 W = 1 J/s): one kilowatt-hour is 1000 watts sustained for 3600 seconds, which equals 3.6 million joules.

Everything runs locally in your browser: there is no account, no upload and no server-side processing. Your numbers stay on your device and are only used to display the conversion results instantly.

Tip: Fill only one field. If multiple fields are filled, the tool asks you to correct it.

Fraction ↔ Decimal ↔ Percentage

Convert a fraction (like 1/4) into a decimal and a percentage, or the other way around, in one click. Everything runs locally in your browser.

Fill exactly one of the fields below:

  • Fraction (example: 1/4, 3/8)
  • Decimal (example: 0.25)
  • Percentage (example: 25 for 25%)

The tool will compute the three representations for you.

Format: a/b (example: 1/2, 3/5, 7/10).
Use a dot for decimals (0.75, 0.333...).
Enter 25 for 25% (not 0.25).

About this fraction, decimal and percentage converter

Fractions, decimals and percentages all describe the same value, but they are used in different contexts. A fraction like 1/4 is common in cooking, measurements, and ratios. A decimal like 0.25 is easier to manipulate in calculators and spreadsheets. A percentage like 25% is often the most readable format for discounts, statistics, interest rates, or progress indicators. This tool helps you switch between these three representations instantly, without doing manual division or mental math.

The workflow is intentionally simple: you fill exactly one field (fraction, decimal, or percentage), then click “Convert values”. The converter computes the other two formats and updates all fields so you can copy the result wherever you need it. This is practical for school exercises (fractions to decimals, decimals to percentages), for recipes that use fractions but require decimal scaling, and for business tasks where a percentage must be converted into a decimal rate (for example, converting 12.5% into 0.125).

When you enter a fraction, the tool parses the a/b format, validates the denominator, and then converts it into a decimal value. It also shows a simplified fraction when possible (for example, 2/4 becomes 1/2). When you start from a decimal, the converter builds a fraction by interpreting the number of decimal places (for instance, 0.75 becomes 75/100 then simplifies to 3/4). Finally, if you enter a percentage, the tool converts it to a decimal by dividing by 100 and then generates the fraction. Percentages are displayed with two decimals for readability, while decimals are displayed with up to six decimals and trimmed to remove unnecessary trailing zeros.

Everything runs locally in your browser. No account is required, no API calls are made, and no data is sent to a server. That makes the converter fast, private, and reliable for quick checks on any device.

Tip: enter only one value at a time. If you type a rounded decimal (like 0.33), the fraction will match that rounded input (33/100), not the exact repeating value (1/3).

Fractions are simplified when possible (example: 2/4 → 1/2). Decimals and percentages are rounded for readability.

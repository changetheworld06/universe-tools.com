Percentage Calculator

Calculate a percentage increase or discount from an initial value. Everything runs locally in your browser.

Enter an initial value and a percentage, then choose whether it’s an increase or a discount. The tool shows the final value and the amount of change.

About this percentage calculator

Percentages are everywhere: discounts in online shops, yearly price changes, taxes, tips, salary adjustments, inflation estimates, and budget planning. This free percentage calculator is designed to help you compute a final value after applying a percentage increase or a percentage discount to an initial number. Instead of doing the math manually, you can enter your starting value, type the percentage, choose the operation (increase or discount), and instantly get the result along with the absolute change amount.

The calculation is straightforward. A percentage change is computed as initial value × (percentage ÷ 100). That amount is then added (for an increase) or subtracted (for a discount). For example, an initial value of 100 with a 20% increase results in 120, while a 20% discount results in 80. Seeing both the final value and the change amount is useful when you want to compare scenarios: “How much do I actually save?” or “What is the real impact of a price rise?” This is common in shopping, invoicing, finance, and everyday decisions.

Typical use cases include calculating a sale price after a discount, estimating VAT or service charges when you know the tax rate, adjusting a budget line by a given percentage, or applying a markup to a cost to get a selling price. In business contexts, it can also help you measure the effect of a percentage-based increase in costs, rent, or wages. If you work with spreadsheets, this tool is a quick alternative when you only need a single calculation.

Privacy matters too. This tool runs 100% locally in your browser: your inputs are not sent to a server, nothing is stored, and no account is required. It’s fast, simple, and works on desktop or mobile. If you need exact accounting results, remember that real invoices may include rounding rules and currency formatting, so you may want to round to 2 decimals and double-check with your official documents.

Tip: if you’re applying multiple successive changes (for example, +10% then -10%), compute each step separately, because percentages apply to the updated value, not the original.

Common mistakes

A common mistake is confusing percentage change with absolute values. Another frequent error is forgetting that percentages are always applied to the current base value, not to an earlier amount.

Limits and alternatives

This tool handles single percentage changes only. It does not compute compounded or successive percentage variations. For complex financial scenarios, spreadsheets or dedicated financial tools may be more appropriate.

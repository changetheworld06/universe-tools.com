How this borrowing capacity calculator works

This free borrowing capacity calculator helps you estimate two key numbers before you start comparing properties or talking to a lender: your maximum affordable monthly loan payment and an indicative maximum loan amount. It is designed as a quick, privacy-friendly tool you can use on desktop or mobile. All computations run locally in your browser, which means the values you enter (income, expenses, interest rate) are not uploaded or stored by this page.

The first step is budgeting. You enter your monthly income and your monthly expenses. Expenses may include recurring fixed costs and/or existing debt payments. Then you choose a target debt-to-income ratio (DTI), expressed as a percentage. The calculator applies this ratio to your income to estimate a maximum “total monthly debt” amount. From that cap, it subtracts your declared expenses to estimate what could remain available for a new loan payment. If the result is zero or negative, it means that with the chosen ratio and your current expenses, there is no room for an additional monthly payment.

The second step converts that estimated monthly payment into a loan principal using a standard fixed-rate loan present-value formula. In simple terms, a longer duration usually increases the principal you can borrow for the same monthly payment, while a higher interest rate usually decreases it. This is an indicative estimate: real borrowing capacity depends on other elements such as borrower profile, lender rules, loan insurance, fees, down payment, taxes, and the required remaining disposable income after all charges.

Use this tool to compare scenarios: adjust the interest rate, change the duration, or test different debt ratios to see how sensitive your project is. For final decisions, always confirm with a qualified professional who can account for your complete situation and local lending requirements.

Disclaimer: simplified estimate only. It is not financial advice and does not replace a lender’s assessment.

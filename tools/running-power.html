About running power zones

Running power zones are a modern way to structure training using objective effort instead of pace or heart rate. Unlike pace, power responds instantly to terrain, wind, fatigue, and gradient changes. This makes it especially useful for trail running, hilly routes, treadmill sessions, or variable conditions where pace alone can be misleading.

The reference value used by most systems is FTP, also known as critical power. It represents the highest power you can sustain for approximately 30 to 60 minutes. From this single number, training zones are defined as percentages of FTP, each corresponding to a physiological intensity.

Lower zones (easy and endurance) are typically used for aerobic base building and recovery runs. Mid zones (tempo and threshold) target sustainable performance and lactate clearance. Higher zones (VO2 max and intervals) focus on maximal oxygen uptake, speed, and neuromuscular power. By training within specific watt ranges, athletes can better control effort and avoid overtraining.

This running power zones calculator uses commonly accepted percentage ranges. However, it is important to understand that zones may vary depending on your watch brand, coaching methodology, or training platform. Always adapt zone boundaries to your personal experience and professional guidance.

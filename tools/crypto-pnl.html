Crypto PnL: how to calculate gains and losses (amount and %)

A crypto PnL calculator helps you understand how your position is performing by computing the difference between what you paid and what your holdings are worth now. “PnL” stands for profit and loss. In everyday investing, people often want two results: the PnL amount (how much you gained or lost in currency) and the PnL percentage (how large the gain or loss is relative to your initial cost). This tool focuses on clear, practical numbers so you can check a trade, compare multiple entries, or track the impact of market moves without needing an exchange dashboard.

The core logic is simple. First, compute the cost basis (what you actually spent): cost basis = entry price × quantity + fees. Fees are optional, but they matter because they reduce net performance. Next, compute your position value: position value = current price × quantity. Finally, compute profit or loss: PnL = position value − cost basis. If this value is positive, you are in profit; if it is negative, you are in loss.

For the percentage, the calculator uses: PnL % = (PnL ÷ cost basis) × 100. This expresses performance relative to what you put in. As a quick example, if your cost basis is 1,000 and your current position value is 1,150, then PnL is +150 and PnL % is +15%. If the value drops to 900, PnL is −100 and PnL % is −10%. These numbers are helpful for comparing positions of different sizes, because percentage returns are scale-independent.

This calculator runs locally in your browser and does not fetch live quotes, which makes it fast and private. Because it is a simple estimator, it does not include taxes, funding rates, interest, liquidation costs, or spread/slippage. For real trading decisions, always confirm the final prices and fees on your exchange. Still, for day-to-day tracking, journaling trades, or quickly checking a scenario, a local PnL calculator is often the fastest way to get clean results.

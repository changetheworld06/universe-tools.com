About this BMI calculator (Body Mass Index)

This free BMI calculator helps you estimate your Body Mass Index (BMI) from two simple measurements: your weight and your height. BMI is a widely used indicator that provides a quick, standardized way to compare body weight relative to height. Because it is simple, it is often used in everyday contexts (fitness, tracking changes over time) and in large-scale health studies. The BMI formula is: BMI = weight (kg) ÷ height² (m²). For example, a person who weighs 70 kg and is 1.75 m tall has a BMI of 70 ÷ (1.75 × 1.75) ≈ 22.9.

Many people use BMI as an initial reference to see an indicative category such as underweight, normal weight, overweight, or obesity. These cutoffs can be helpful for a high-level overview, but BMI has important limitations. It does not directly measure body fat, body composition, muscle mass, bone density, or fat distribution. As a result, a very muscular person may have a high BMI without having excess body fat, and BMI may not reflect health risks equally for all ages and populations. In other words, BMI is a useful screening indicator, but it is not a diagnosis.

This calculator intentionally focuses on speed and privacy. The computation runs locally in your browser: no account is required, and your inputs are not stored or transmitted by this page. You can use it on desktop or mobile for quick checks, to follow a trend over time, or to validate a BMI value from another source.

If you have health concerns, recent weight changes, or you need personalized guidance (for example, during pregnancy, for children/teens, or for medical conditions), it’s best to consult a qualified healthcare professional. BMI can be one piece of information among others (waist circumference, clinical history, lifestyle, lab results) to get a more accurate picture.

Note: the displayed category is indicative only and should not be used as a medical diagnosis.
